Jean-Michel Basquiat, was a Haitian-American artist, musician, and producer from Brooklyn NYC.

He first achieved notoriety as part of SAMO, an informal graffiti group who wrote enigmatic epigrams in the cultural hotbed of the Lower East Side of Manhattan during the late 1970s where the hip-hop, post-punk and street art movements had coalesced.

1979

Basquiat appeared on the live public-access television cable TV show TV Party. Basquiat made regular appearances on the show over the next few years. That same year, Basquiat formed the noise rock band Test Pattern – which was later renamed Gray – which played at Arleen Schloss's open space, "Wednesdays at A's", where in October 1979 Basquiat showed, among others, his SAMO color Xerox work.

1980

Basquiat starred in O'Brien's independent film Downtown 81. That same year, he met Andy Warhol at a restaurant. The two artists later collaborated.

He also appeared in the 1981 Blondie music video "Rapture" as a nightclub disc jockey. The music video was the first rap video ever broadcast on MTV.

1981

In September Basquiat joined the Annina Nosei gallery and worked in a basement below the gallery toward his first one-man show, which took place in March 1981 with great success.

In December 1981, René Ricard published "The Radiant Child" in Artforum magazine, which brought Basquiat to the attention of the art world.

1982

In March 1982 he worked in Modena, Italy and from November, Basquiat worked in Venice, California. He brought along his girlfriend, then unknown aspiring singer Madonna.

During this time he took considerable interest in the work that Robert Rauschenberg was producing. In 1982, Basquiat also worked briefly with musician and artist David Bowie.

1983

In 1983, Basquiat produced a 12" rap single featuring hip-hop artists Rammellzee and K-Rob.

Billed as Rammellzee vs. K-Rob, the single contained two versions of the same track:"Beat Bop".

1984-86

Warhol and Basquiat worked on a series of collaborative paintings. Basquiat responded to the abstract, stylized logos with his oppositional graffiti style. Basquiat often painted in expensive Armani suits and would even appear in public in the same paint-splattered clothes.

When Andy Warhol died on February 22, 1987, Basquiat became increasingly isolated, and his heroin addiction and depression grew more severe. Despite an attempt at sobriety during a trip to Maui, Hawaii, he died on August 12, 1988, of a heroin overdose at his art studio on Great Jones Street in Manhattan's NoHo neighborhood. He was 27.
